MusicGen-Rhythm: Extending MusicGen
with Rhythmic Structure Conditioning

This work extends the MusicGen framework by introducing rhythmic and temporal structure conditioning derived directly from audio. Existing music audio generation approaches struggle to capture deeper musical structures because MusicGen relies solely on text conditioning. Prior extensions such as MusicGen–Melody and MusicGen–Chord improve coherence but depend on symbolic inputs. Our method extracts rhythmic features from log-magnitude mel-spectrograms, including energy variations, spectral flux, and RMS. A CNN encoder converts these features into structure embeddings aligned to the audio timeline. These embeddings are fused with text representations using weighted gating or lightweight cross-attention. The combined conditioning is passed to the Transformer decoder without retraining the MusicGen backbone. This approach enhances rhythmic consistency and temporal coherence in generated music.
